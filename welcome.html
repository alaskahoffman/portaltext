portaltext is now active in your browser. The sections below show every way the extension can surface context for what you're reading. Try each one right here on this page — your tooltips light up as you hover.

Hover any link.

Move your cursor over a link, wait a beat, and a summary opens — written by an AI that reads the linked page and the page you're already on, then stitches them together.

try: hover a name below

Jorge Luis Borges spent his last decade nearly blind, dictating his stories from memory. He once said reading is more important than writing — a sentiment Italo Calvino echoed in his own essays. Both belong to a strange lineage that runs through hypertext fiction, books that fork at every junction, decades before the web.

Hover a PDF.

PDFs and other heavyweight pages need a little more help — portaltext reads what it can and surfaces a summary the same way it would for a regular page. A bigger lift, so it draws a little more from your daily allowance.

try: hover the PDF link

The classic example: Attention Is All You Need (PDF, arXiv 1706.03762) — the 2017 paper that introduced the Transformer architecture. Hover it and the tooltip reads the PDF directly.

Citations, auto-detected.

arXiv IDs, DOIs, PMIDs — portaltext recognizes academic citation patterns even when they're not wrapped in a link, and turns each one into a hoverable portal to the paper itself.

try: hover any identifier

The Transformer paper lives at arXiv:1706.03762, and its 2024 follow-up at DOI:10.48550/arXiv.2401.04088. For biomedical work, try PMID:31735529 — a study on nutritional psychiatry.

17 languages.

Summaries can be written in any of 17 languages, regardless of the source page's language. Set it once in the popup → Settings → Tooltip language, then everything from English Wikipedia to Spanish news reads in your chosen language.

try: change language in popup, then hover

Read about Borges en español, 宮崎駿 in Japanese, or Walter Benjamin auf deutsch — set your tooltip language and the summaries arrive in it.

Make anything a portal.

No link? Make one. Select any text on any page, right-click, and choose "Make hoverable with portaltext". The selection becomes a portal you can hover for context — even on text that wasn't originally linked.

try: select a phrase below → right-click → "Make hoverable"

The longest river in the world, the Nile, runs north — a curiosity that puzzled travelers for centuries. Try selecting "the Nile", or any phrase that catches your eye in this paragraph, right-click it, and turn it into a portal. The tooltip will arrive with the context, even without a link.
